Panopticon: a system for searching video quickly and accurately
December 12, 2016
TL;DR: Inexpensive, high resolution video sensors are deployed in increasing numbers in a wide variety of applications ranging from video surveillance to self-driving cars. Simultaneously, convolutional neural networks (CNNs) have become the the dominant tool for deriving semantic information from this kind of visual data but are in turn highly computationally expensive. To illustrate, a webcam costs as little as $2, but the hardware needed to actually evaluate a CNN on the webcam video stream in real time can cost $1200 or more. Read more...
